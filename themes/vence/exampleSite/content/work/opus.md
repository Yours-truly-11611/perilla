When three regional arts organisations — a concert series, a gallery programme, and a residency scheme — merged into a single entity, they faced a naming and positioning challenge of unusual complexity.

Each of the three had loyal audiences. Each had its own identity, history, and sense of self. The merger was practical but potentially destructive to all three communities if handled as a simple rebrand.

Our strategy work ran for four months before a name was chosen. We facilitated workshops with over sixty stakeholders — artists, board members, funders, audiences. We mapped values, tensions, and the territory each organisation had earned. The positioning we arrived at — *a foundation for the long work of culture* — gave the new entity its own gravity rather than borrowing from what came before.

Opus launched eighteen months ago. Membership has grown 34% across all three legacy communities. The three programmes continue under the Opus name and are outperforming their pre-merger metrics on every measure.
